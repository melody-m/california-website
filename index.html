10 Incredible Stops on a Pacific Coast Highway Road Trip

The Pacific Coast Highway (PCH) was first started in the 1930s, and remains one of the most astoundingly scenic roads in the world, spreading over 600 miles (900 kilometers) of California’s beautiful coastlines. This very popular roadtrip roads is a fantastic opportunity to discover California, admiring the magnificiant landscapes, pioneer outposts, surfing villages, while also making stops in major cities. Theoretically, the PCH goes all the way from San Diego to Vancouver, so quite a long way! However, we've created an itinary that goes from Los Angeles to San Francisco, with 10 incredible stops on the way, making it an easier roadtrip if you're flying in and out of California.

Starting point : Los Angeles

There is a lot to see and do in Los Angeles. We recommend that you stop in Marina Del Rey, just a stone throw away from the airport, one of the biggest marina in the world, full of incredible yachts. Then head on towards the quirky Venice, and stroll around the beautiful canals, a hidden gem just a few steps from beach but well worth a detour. Walk along the Hollywood Walk of Fame, drive down Sunset Boulevard and stroll down Rodeo Drive. You could check out the awesome views of the Hollywood sign from Griffith Observatory.

Santa Monica

With its “Baywatch” beaches, white sand and old-fashioned Pier, it is a really nice spot to spend a day. It is quite easy to walk around in Santa Monica, you can also hire a bike, you'll see lots of people strolling by the beach, all the way from Venice beach. The Pier and 3rd Street Promenade are always buzzing with loads of activities and plenty of shops. If you walk further away from the center towards the north, you'll encounter incredible houses.

Malibu

Malibu has incredible beaches, more natural than the slightly artificial look of the Santa Monica ones. Follow the signs to Point Dume and you’ll find a beautiful sandy beach, headlands, cliffs and rocky coves. Take a walk around Malibu and you’ll find piers and super healthy restaurants. Exit Malibu via the stunning Topanga Canyon – a popular weekend hideaway for Hollywood superstars for years. Stay on Route 27 and you’ll come across offbeat stores, diners and ramshackle saloons along the roadside. Get out at the top for an awesome view over the San Fernando Valley at the top of Topanga Overlook.

Santa Barbara

Santa Barbara has a really nice vibe, a perfect mix between casual beach, history and city attractions. This is a city filled with adobe homes, Spanish Colonial Revival architecture and mansions, you can learn more about the city’s colonial history at the Old Mission Santa Barbara. Shop and explore the city’s arts district known as the Funk Zone as well as the multiple crafty shops. Animal and flora lovers will enjoy Santa Barbara Zoo, Santa Barbara Botanic Garden and Lotusland. The pier hosts plenty of restaurants with plenty of seafood choice.

Morro Bay

Morro Bay is a seaside fishing village with bustling waterfront and gorgeous natural setting. Home to world-famous Morro Rock, this seaside destination serves as the backdrop to an abundance of year-round activities like boating, kayaking, surfing, golfing, hiking and biking. At Morro Bay State Park, you may see any number of wild animals including blue herons, monarch butterflies, hummingbirds and sea otters. The park offers trails, a small museum and picnic benches, making it a perfect road trip stop for when you need to stretch your legs.

Cambria and San Simeon

On the way into Cambria, stop at the Piedras Blancas Friends of the Elephant Seal rookery, where crowds of elephant seals mate and fight (don't get too close, they can be dangerous) Continue to the charming seaside village, home to rocky shorelines, bluffs and tide pools teeming with sea life. When you hit San Simeon, make your way up to Hearst Castle on The Enchanted Hill and you can travel back in time as you make your way through more than 250,000 acres of rooms, suites, cottages, kitchens and grounds.

Big Sur

To get to the Big Sur you’ll travel over the Bixby Bridge – one of the highest bridges of its kind in the world, soars 260 feet above the bottom of a steep canyon carved by Bixby Creek and one of the most photographed bridges on the West Coast. There are many beautiful sights along the Big Sur – Pfeiffer Beach, McWay Falls, Point Sur State Historic Park, Ragged Point – it’ll be hard to get any driving done with all the lookout points. If you have time, take a few days to relax along the route.

Monterey Bay

On your way to Monterey, check out the stunning Point Lobos State Natural Reserve and chill at the Old Fisherman’s Wharf. If you have time, stop by Carmel-by-the-Sea a nice seaside village with shops, cafes and restaurants. Monterey is well known for its Aquarium (yes the one in Saving Nemo!) situated in Cannery Row where you can also find shops and restaurants. Constantly surprising with new exhibits and programs, and with a dedication to marine education and conservation, the aquarium is ranked among the top attractions in the world and well worth a visit.

Santa Cruz

Santa Cruz is a friendly beach town with gorgeous redwood trees and waves that surfers from all over flock to ride. Founded in 1907, the Santa Cruz Boardwalk has an old-school Americana vibe with its old-fashioned roller coasters and arcade. The Mystery Spot – a collection of strange phenomena from the last 70 years – also makes for an entertaining few hours. The Wharf is a great place to start for restaurants and shops and if you have the time you can go on a whale watching boat or fishing expedition.
